<p><p>Hospital Real-time Location Systems (RTLS) use various technologies to track assets, staff, and patients within healthcare facilities. RFID (Radio-Frequency Identification) employs tags and readers for precise location tracking. Wi-Fi utilizes existing network infrastructure for positioning, offering flexibility and coverage. Ultrasound systems use sound waves for accurate location data, ideal for smaller environments. Infrared systems rely on light signals for tracking, requiring line-of-sight. Additionally, other emerging technologies, such as Bluetooth and visual tracking, contribute to enhancing operational efficiency in hospitals.</p></p>
