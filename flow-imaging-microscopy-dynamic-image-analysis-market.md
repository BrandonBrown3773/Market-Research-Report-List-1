<p><p>Flow Imaging Microscopy (FIM) is a dynamic image analysis technique used to characterize particulate materials by capturing high-resolution images of particles in motion. The market for FIM is divided into two primary types: wet dispersion and dry dispersion. Wet dispersion involves suspending particles in a liquid medium, facilitating the analysis of particle size and shape in a controlled environment. In contrast, dry dispersion analyzes particles in a dry state, allowing for the study of material properties without the influence of solvents. Both methods provide insights into particle behavior and distribution in various industries.</p></p>
