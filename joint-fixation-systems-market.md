<p><p>The Joint Fixation Systems market encompasses various types of fixation devices used in orthopedic surgeries and trauma care. Plates are flat metal components that stabilize fractures by being fixed to the bone. Screws are used to secure plates or directly bind bone fragments together. Rods provide support for longer bone alignments, often in spinal procedures. Pins are used to hold bone fragments in place during healing, while wires are flexible elements used to secure fractured bones or soft tissues.</p></p>
